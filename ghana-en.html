Implementing Timber Leglity Assurance Systems

A guide to comply with timber legality requirements in Cameroon and to support due diligence

GHANA

Ghana’s Timber legality requirements

Ghana has signed a Voluntary Partnership Agreement with the European Union. The definition of legality and the tools used to ensure the legality of organizations and individuals in the forestry sector are set out in Annex II of the Agreement. It is a legal matrix that encompasses seven principles: source of timber, Timber Rights Allocation, Timber Harvesting Operations, Transportation, Processing, Trade and Fiscal Obligations. By being aware of legal requirements in Ghana highlighted below, law enforcement agencies and importers can implement control measures to promote timber legally harvested, transported and traded. Importers and verification agencies may refer to or use this definition in implementing due diligence.

Suggestions for developing and implementing due diligence for importers

Due diligence and complexity

Due diligence is the compliance of organizations and individuals with legal regulations in terms of tracking timber origin and proving the legality of traded timber products.
The scope of a due diligence system depends on the number and the complexity of supply chains, e.g. multi-middleman and/or many organizations and individuals participating in the supply chain, many timber species and timber owners.

Developing due diligence systems

Develop a system with leadership commitment, policies, procedures, organizational structures, templates/checklists and key responsible persons.
Gather information from supply chains.
Evaluate supply chains and gaps/risk assessments.
Implement risk mitigation
Set up and store documents.

Implementing due diligence

Evaluate the validity of documentation in term of date, period and authorization.
Check that each document is up to date, has been signed/stamped by the authorized agencies.
Cross-check consistent and logical within itself, with other provided documents and with actual products (species, volume, location of harvest, etc.) being sold/exported.
The necessary measures: require further information from suppliers; conduct stakeholder consultation; test timber; send staff to check timber sources; and/or hire third parties for on-site checks.

The following documents are selected in accordance with Ghana’s legal definition and/or legal requirements in different stages of the supply chain.

HARVESTING TIMBER
Harvesting rights

Certificate of business registration

Certificate of business registration is signed and stamped by the business registration legal entity. Check the signature, seal of the company registrant or designee, business name, registration date on the certificate.

Timber Utilization Contract (TUC)

Timber Utilization Contract is signed, stamped and issued by the Minister of Land and Natural Resources to all timber cutting organizations (not belonging to private plantations). Check the TUC approved by the National Assembly, the validity of term, signature, and seal of the Minister on the contract.

Letter of approval for production under a Restricted Timber Species License

A letter of approval is signed and stamped by the Director of the Regional Department of Forest Service, issued to the owner of the felling timber, excluding salvaged logging. Check the owner's name, signature, seal, number of trees, tree location on the approval letter.

Develop and approve management plan

Social Responsibility Agreement (SRA)

The social responsibility agreement is signed by representatives of harvesting companies, communities, witnesses (District Boards) and staff of the Forest Service. Check signatures of FSD staff and stakeholders, work plan for SRA.

Assure compliance with customary rights, rights of indigenous people

Social Responsibility Agreement (SRA)

The social responsibility agreement is signed by representatives of harvesting companies, communities, witnesses (District Boards) and staff of the Forest Service. Check signatures of FSD staff and stakeholders, work plan for SRA.

Assure compliance with the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES).

CITES export Permit

The CITES export permit is signed, stamped and issued by the Department of Wildlife under the Forestry Commission to the exporter of CITES timber species. Check the validity, signature and seal, name of the timber owner on the permit.

Assure compliance with tax and fee regulations

Tax payment certificate

A certificate of tax payment is signed, stamped and issued by the Director of the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) or an authorized person, and issued to the business entity that has fulfilled its tax obligations. Check the taxpayer's name, GRA embossed logo, validity of date, signature and seal on the certificate.

Print out of Timber Right Holders Accounts statement

Print out of Timber Right Holders Accounts statement is signed, stamped and issued by the accountant of the Forest Service at the district level. Check the timber owner's name, reporting time, and the most recent balance on the statement.

TRADE & TRANSPORT
Implement full compliance with procedures and timber export inspection

Export permit

The export permit is signed and stamped by the Director of the Timber Industries Development Division (TIDD) of the Forestry Commission, and is issued to all timber exporters (by sea and by land) except for export sample shipments ( less than 1 cubic meter). Check signature, seal of TIDD, date of signing, compare with name of timber owner.

PROCESSING
Assure compliance with regulations on business registration

Certificate of registration and renewal

The certificate of registration and renewal is signed, stamped and issued by the Chief Inspector of the Department of Factory Inspectorate, Ministry of Employment and Labor Relations. Check the term, signature, seal, nature of work, legal entity name on the certificate.

Assure compliance with environmental and social regulations

Approval of Environmental Impact Statement (Environmental Impact Assessment and Preliminary Environmental Impact Assessment)

Environmental impact statement is signed and approved by the Director of the Department of Environmental Protection. Check the time of assessment, signature and stamp, the name of the project owner on the approval decision. The regulations can be viewed online at http://www.epa.gov.gh/epa/regulations

Environmental Permit

The environmental permit is signed and stamped by the Director of the Department of Environmental Protection or authorized to the Head of the Department of Environmental Assessment and Audit. An environmental permit is considered a temporary permit, which should be replaced by an environmental certificate. Check the term, signature and seal, the name of the project owner on the license.

CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES
Assure compliance with regulations on labor, occupational safety and health

Payment certificate by the Social Security and National Insurance Trust (SSNIT)

SSNIT payment certificate is signed and stamped by Director or Branch SSNIT as authorized by General Director of SSNIT, issued to all employers. Check the number of workers, the appropriate information on the enterprise registration certificate, signature, and seal of SSNIT.

Labor contract

The labor contract is drawn up by the company and signed by the employer and the employee. Check the contract contents, information, signatures and seals are complete and accurate.
